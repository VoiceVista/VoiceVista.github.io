Editor's Viewpoint

Rethinking Access, Reimagining Equity

By Editorial Desk • Published Feb 22, 2026 • 8 Min Read

India’s reservation system was established with a clear and powerful objective — to promote social justice and provide opportunities to communities that have historically faced structural disadvantages. Over the decades, this system has enabled millions to access education, employment, and dignity that might otherwise have remained out of reach.

However, an important and sensitive question deserves thoughtful discussion: Are the benefits within the reserved category consistently reaching those who need them the most?

The Emerging Concern

Within any large social group, economic and educational differences naturally exist. Over time, some families within reserved categories have achieved stability, financial security, and access to quality education. This progress is a positive outcome of policy support and personal effort.

Yet, a concern arises when the same relatively advantaged sections continue to access reserved opportunities across generations, while individuals from more vulnerable backgrounds struggle to compete. Access to better schooling and urban exposure creates a significant advantage — even within reserved quotas.

"Reservation is a tool for social transformation. Its strength lies in its ability to uplift those who are truly left behind."

Moving Forward

The goal should not be division or blame, but refinement. Constructive dialogue can help identify ways to improve access for the most economically disadvantaged and strengthen verification mechanisms to ensure fairness.
